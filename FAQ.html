Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about blood donation, eligibility, and the donation process.

Who can donate blood?
Generally, you can donate blood if you:
  • Are at least 17 years old (16 in some states with parental consent)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Are in good health
  • Have not donated blood in the last 56 days
There are specific eligibility requirements related to medical conditions, medications, and travel history. Our medical staff will evaluate your eligibility during the donation process.
How often can I donate blood?
You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). If you're donating platelets, you can donate every 7 days up to 24 times per year. For plasma, you can donate every 28 days, and for double red cells, every 112 days.
Can I donate if I have high blood pressure?
Yes, in most cases. If your blood pressure is below 180/100 at the time of donation, you are usually eligible to donate. If you're taking medication for high blood pressure, you should be stable on your medication.
How long does the donation process take?
The entire process takes about an hour, though the actual blood donation only takes 8-10 minutes. This includes registration, mini-physical, donation, and refreshments afterward.
Does donating blood hurt?
Most donors report feeling only a brief pinch when the needle is inserted. The actual donation process is typically painless. Our staff is trained to make the experience as comfortable as possible.
What types of donation are available?
We offer several types of donations:
  • Whole Blood: The most common type of donation
  • Platelets: Takes longer but provides critical components for cancer patients
  • Plasma: The liquid portion of blood used for various treatments
  • Double Red Cells: Donates twice the amount of red cells
Your blood type and local needs determine which donation type might be best for you.
What should I do after donating blood?
After donating:
  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Avoid strenuous physical activity for 24 hours
  • Keep the bandage on for several hours
  • Eat healthy, iron-rich foods
  • If you feel dizzy, sit or lie down until you feel better
How long does it take to replenish the blood I donated?
Your body replaces the blood volume (plasma) within 24 hours. Red blood cells are replaced by your bone marrow within 4-6 weeks, which is why you need to wait at least 8 weeks between whole blood donations.
Who benefits from blood donations?
Blood donations help many people, including:
  • Accident and trauma victims
  • Cancer patients
  • People undergoing surgery
  • Those with blood disorders like sickle cell anemia
  • Women with pregnancy complications
  • Premature babies
One donation can save up to three lives!
What blood types are most needed?
All blood types are needed, but:
  • O Negative: Universal donor for red cells, can be given to anyone
  • O Positive: Most common blood type
  • AB Negative: Rarest blood type
  • AB Positive: Universal recipient, can receive any type
Type O negative is particularly needed for emergencies when there's no time to determine a patient's blood type.
Is there a blood shortage?
Blood supplies vary throughout the year, with shortages common during holidays, summer, and winter months. Only about 3% of eligible people donate blood, which is why we always need more donors. Every donation makes a significant difference in maintaining a stable blood supply.
